About Mrs. J


Hello! I am excited to tell you a little bit about myself!

Growing up in St. Peters, I went to school in the Fort Zumwalt School District. After graduation, I attended Missouri State University, where I worked towards a degree in Elementary Education. I lived in Springfield, Missouri, and was a part of a Christian sorority called Gamma Alpha Lambda. In 2012, I graduated and moved back to St. Louis. During that summer, I worked at the St. Louis Science Center as a summer camp instructor. From there, I taught fourth grade at Point Elementary School in the Mehlville School District. For the next five years, I taught sixth-grade ELA at Oakville Middle School, also in the Mehlville School District. During the 2017-2018 school year, I was awarded Teacher of the Year for both my school and the entire Mehlville District. Next year, I will begin teaching sixth-grade ELA in the Fort Zumwalt School District at North Middle School.

In 2013, I married the love of my life, Hunter, and we moved into a small fixer-upper house in Affton, Missouri. We spent a few years remodeling and doing a lot of home renovations. I learned a lot about framing, flooring, painting and more! We did most of the work ourselves to save money. In 2017, we sold our house and moved to O'Fallon to be closer to my family.

There are many things I like to do for fun. On the weekends, we like to go fishing in our backyard lake. We bring our dog, Layla, with us. We rescued her in 2013 from Love a Golden Rescue. She is the sweetest! I also love to paint, draw, sketch, and journal. As a reading teacher, I also love curling up with a good book on a rainy day. Some of my favorite authors include Clive Cussler and Dan Brown. We love to travel, too! Every few years, we go to Cancun, Mexico, or Playa del Carmen. Another passion of mine is technology. I am currently working on a Masters Degree in Learning Systems Design and Development through Mizzou. Learning to code is hard, yet fun! I'm always trying to incorporate fun tech tools into the classroom.

Enough about me, hopefully I will have the opportunity to learn about you as well!
