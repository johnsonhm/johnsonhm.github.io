Hannah Johnson's Basic Webpage

Option 1: ELA6

My first idea is to develop a website for my sixth-grade ELA students to use on a daily basis. It would be a central location for all of my classroom resources and activities. All the pages would revolve around the idea of gamifying my classroom. Here are some page ideas:

  • Quest Menu: A list of quests. One for each reading and writing skill.
  • Meet Your Team: Directions and resources for students to find their home base, come up with a team name, complete a teambuilder activity, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log.
  • Prepare Your Mind: Directions and resources for students to read about growth mindset, study habits, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log.
  • Gather Intel: Directions and resources for students to learn about a specific reading or writing skill, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log. (Each skill would have its own "Gather Intel" page.)
  • Battle Practice: Directions and resources for students to play and practice a specific reading or writing skill, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log. (Each skill would have its own "Battle Practice" page.)
  • Regroup: Directions and resources for students to go back and correct their previous assessment, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log.
  • Learn from Leaders: Directions and resources for students to research a STEM career, describe how it uses that particular reading or writing skill, participate in a discussion, and fill out an adventure log.
  • Create a Challenge: Directions and resources for students to create their own practice activity for the particular skill and fill out an adventure log.
  • Defeat the Boss: Directions and resources for students to take an assessment for the particular skill and fill out an adventure log.
  • Reading Guide: Directions and resources for students to browse book club books, choose their top three, look at an overview, ask and answer questions by posting to FlipGrid, take a Reading Counts Quiz, and explore their books.
  • Writing Guide: Directions and resources for students to work through each step of the writing process.
  • Adventure Log: Link to a Google Form that students fill out at the end of class where they summarize the day's learning.
  • Leaderboard: Display of each class's total experience points (XP) as well as the top ten students (using avatar names) for each class.
  • Item Shop: Display a list of items and rewards students can "purchase" by trading in their XP.
  • Visitors: Information for parents or teachers.

Option 2: Agents of Change

My second idea is to develop a website for parents, teachers, and other community members to inform them about Agents of Change. This is a year-long STEM project I created for my ELA classroom. All the pages would have revolve around the details of this project. Here are some page ideas:

  • Overview: Introduce the mission and steps of the project. (How can we use our communication, critical thinking, and collaboration skills to create something that makes our world more awesome?
  • Step 1: List problems. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 2: Choose a topic. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 3: Form teams. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 4: Initial research. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 5: Invite an expert, or community partner to help collaborate. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 6: Project idea and overview. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 7: Project plan of action and timeline. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 8: Request permissions and supplies. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 9: Advertise and promote your project. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 10: Do it, collect data, document evidence of journey. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 11: Analyze impact of project on community. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Steo 12: Prepare to share. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 13: Invitations for Celebration Convention. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 14: Practice presentations. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • Step 15: Celebration Convention. (Information, pictures, lesson plans, resources, etc.)
  • In the News: Articles, photos, and links to news coverage of this project. I will include information about Samsung's Solve for Tomorrow contest.
  • FAQ / Tips: Answers to frequently asked questions, or tips for other teachers to implement this project.
